What is Perimenopause?

It's the transition to menopause, which can start in your late 30s and last up to 10 years. It's not just "hot flushes".

The Timeline

Perimenopause is the time leading up to menopause (12 months without a period). Hormones fluctuate wildly during this time.

Common Symptoms

  • Hot Flushes & Night Sweats
  • Brain Fog & Anxiety
  • Joint Pain & Fatigue
  • Irregular Periods

Long-Term Health

Estrogen protects your heart and bones. As levels drop, risks for Osteoporosis and Cardiovascular Disease increase.

The South Asian Experience

Research shows that South Asian women often experience menopause differently, both physically and culturally.

Different Symptoms

While hot flushes are common, South Asian women frequently report higher levels of:

  • Anxiety & Panic Attacks
  • Palpitations
  • Severe Fatigue & Body Aches
  • Visual Disturbances

Cultural Barriers

Stigma often prevents women from seeking help:

  • Silence: Topics like libido and menstruation are often taboo.
  • Family Dynamics: "Male-dominated" environments can lead to lack of support.
  • Misdiagnosis: It takes 45% of BAME women "many" appointments to get a diagnosis compared to 30% of white women.
  • Duty: Feeling a loss of identity as the "woman of the house" due to fatigue.

HRT: Fact vs. Fiction

Hormone Replacement Therapy is the first-line treatment for menopausal symptoms. Let's clear up the confusion.

Myth

"HRT is dangerous and causes cancer."

Fact

For most women, benefits outweigh risks. Modern "body-identical" HRT has a very low risk profile. Transdermal estrogen (patches/gels) has no increased clot risk.

Myth

"I should just put up with it."

Fact

Suffering isn't necessary. HRT protects against heart disease and osteoporosis if started early (within 10 years of menopause).

Myth

"Natural remedies are better."

Fact

Most herbal alternatives lack proven efficacy. Lifestyle changes (diet, exercise) help, but HRT is the most effective treatment for symptoms.
